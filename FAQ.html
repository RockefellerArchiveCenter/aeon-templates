About RACcess

What is RACcess?

RACcess is the system you use to request materials held at the Rockefeller Archive Center as well as track the history of requests you’ve received previously.

What can I do in RACcess?

With this system you can submit requests for archival materials or library books at the RAC, either in advance of a visit, or while at the RAC. You can also submit requests for scans or photocopies of archival materials, track the status of requests, and access detailed information about past requests.

Your Account

Why do I need a username and password to use RACcess?

Your username and password allows us to give you secure access to information about your requests. We can also enable you to edit your personal information such as your address or phone number.

Your username can be anything you like, such as your name, abbreviations, or an alphanumeric code. Your password also can be anything you like. We recommend that you follow good security practice and choose a password that is different from those you use to access other systems. However, no one but you will know your password, not even RAC staff. The password you set is stored encrypted.

May I create an account in advance?

Yes, all researchers are encouraged to create an account online prior to their arrival.

What if I forget my password?

In the event that you forget your password, please click on the Forgot Password link on the logon page to reset it. For security reasons, RAC staff do not have access to your password and cannot tell you what it is.

How long is my request information kept?

We may retain information we collect from you in connection with your use of a RACcess account indefinitely.

How long is my account active?

Your account is active indefinitely. However, if it has been more than 365 days since you registered or last updated your account information, you will be asked to update your contact and affiliation information the next time you login.

What does "Subscribe to Alerts Feed" mean?

Subscribing to the alerts feeds allows you to get alerts from us sent to your RSS reader.

Requesting Material

May I request material in advance?

Yes, researchers may submit requests at any time after completing the online registration form.

How many items may I request?

There are no limits on the number of items you may request in your account. However, depending on the sizes of the boxes requested, we can only bring a maximum of 10 boxes at a time to you in the reading room.

How can I request material?

Researchers with a scheduled research appointment can request to view items in the reading room through DIMES. In select cases, materials you wish to see in the reading room may not be represented in DIMES. You will need to submit a manual request for these materials.

Can I see a record of the items I have requested?

Yes, researchers can see a record of all requested and viewed items in their research account.

Can I request photocopies and digital images through my research account?

Yes, researchers can request photocopies, PDFs or high-resolution scans through their research account.

How can I request copies?

Researchers can request copies of archival material through DIMES. In select cases, materials you want to have duplicated may not be represented in DIMES. You will need to submit a manual request for these materials. For more information, see our Duplication Procedures and Fee Schedule.

Planning a Visit

Where can I find information about planning my research visit?

For general information about visiting the Rockefeller Archive Center, see our “Information for Researchers” page.
